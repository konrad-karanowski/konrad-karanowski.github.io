About me

    My name is Konrad Karanowski. I am a student of Computer Science at the Wrocław University of Science and Technology and an aspiring machine learning researcher from Poland. I love discovering new things and studying various phenomena and properties of machine learning algorithms.

    I am a co-author of many scientific publications (including some in journals with an Impact Factor over 4.8) covering areas such as machine learning in medicine, few-shot learning, continuous learning and personalization in NLP.

    Currently, I work as a machine learning specialist at the Wrocław-based company BioCam, where I deal with deep learning algorithms supporting capsule endoscopy.

    In my spare time, I love to listen to music (especially from Sibellius, Karłowicz and John Williams) as well as composing.

Maciej Zamorski, Michał Stypułkowski, Konrad Karanowski, Tomasz Trzciński, Maciej Zięba

Computer Vision And Image Understanding | Journal Paper | 2023

HyperShot: Few-Shot Learning by kernel hypernetworks

Marcin Sendera, Marcin Przewięźlikowski, Konrad Karanowski, Maciej Zięba, Jacek Tabor, Przemysław Spurek

Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision | Conference Paper | 2023

This work proposes HyperShot the fusion of kernels and hypernetwork paradigm, which aims to switch the classification module parameters depending on the task’s embedding and relies on relations between embeddings of the support examples instead of direct feature values provided by the backbone models.

Link

Data Augmentation for Morphological Analysis of Histopathological Images Using Deep Learning

Martin Tabakov, Konrad Karanowski, Adam R Chlopowiec, Adrian B Chlopowiec, Mikolaj Kasperek

International Conference on Computational Collective Intelligence | Conference Paper | 2022

In this study, we introduce a data augmentation procedure for histopathology image classification. This is an extension to our previous research, in which we showed the possibility to apply deep learning for morphological analysis of tumour cells.

What If Ground Truth Is Subjective? Personalized Deep Neural Hate Speech Detection

Kamil Kanclerz, Marcin Gruza, Konrad Karanowski, Julita Bielaniewicz, P. Milkowski, Jan Kocoń, Przemyslaw Kazienko

NLPERSPECTIVES | Conference Paper | 2022

The experiments revealed that all models leveraging user preferences in any case provide significantly better results than most frequently used generalized approaches, which supports the overall observation that personalized models should always be considered in all subjective NLP tasks, including hate speech detection.

Link

Morphological Analysis of Histopathological Images Using Deep Learning

Artur Zawisza, Martin Tabakov, Konrad Karanowski, Krzysztof Galus

International Conference on Computational Collective Intelligence | Conference Paper | 2021

In this study, we introduce a morphological analysis of segmented tumour cells from histopathology images concerning the recognition of cell overlapping. The main research problem considered is to distinguish how many cells are located in a structure, which is composed of overlapping cells.
